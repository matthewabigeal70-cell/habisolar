FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions about switching to solar energy? Find answers to our most common inquiries below.

How long do HABI SOLAR panels last?

Our monocrystalline panels are engineered to last upwards of 30 years. Every solar system we install includes a 25-year performance warranty guaranteeing the panels will operate at at least 85% of their original capacity.

What happens on cloudy or rainy days?

Solar panels still generate electricity on overcast days by capturing indirect sunlight, producing about 10% to 25% of their peak output. For uninterrupted power, we recommend integrating our smart battery storage units which store surplus solar energy for rainy weather.

Are there government tax incentives for installing solar?

Yes! Federal and state incentives can significantly offset installation costs. Under the current Investment Tax Credit (ITC), homeowners can deduct up to 30% of total solar installation costs directly from their federal tax liability.

How does Net Metering work?

Net Metering is a utility billing system where surplus power generated by your panels is fed back into the municipal electric grid. Your electric company issues credits to your monthly utility account, meaning you only pay for the net energy consumed.
